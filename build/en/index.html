First Tool:

The Recommended Foods List

One of the main ways of slowing down the disease's progression, is by reducing sugar and starch intake as much as possible. To make it much easier, tens, or maybe even hundreds of hours were put into researching the nutrtional values of many, many different day to day items, and compiling them into a list.

This tool is, basically, a very long list of foods, with their sugar and carbohydrate contents attached, as well as their glycemic index, and glycemic load, with the latter being the most important, and (of course) somewhat rare piece of data.

To improve the tool and the traction it gets, it has a nice content contribution feature: you can request/provide foods you want to be added to the list - and if they're added, you can get on the content contribution leaderboards, and with enough content contributions, even reach first place ðŸ˜‰

As of writing this, it already contains about 200(!) different items, with many more on the way :)

Features:

  • Lots of data about different items
  • Colors for intuitive and easy navigation
  • Filtering data by search
  • Ordering by 7 different categories
  • Ability to request/provide items of your own (Don't worry about accuracy, everything is double-checked ðŸ™‚)
  • List of content contribution leaderboards
